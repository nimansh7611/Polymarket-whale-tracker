How markets are scanned
Polymarket has thousands of markets but most have little to no activity. This tool fetches the top 100 markets sorted by 24-hour volume, then filters out anything below $5,000 in daily volume — leaving only markets with real money moving through them right now. Each market is then scored on five heuristics that can indicate unusual or informed trading activity:
Volume spike (35pts)
More than 40% of a market's all-time volume traded in the last 24 hours — suggests a sudden rush of informed buyers.
Extreme price (25pts)
Price above 75¢ or below 25¢ with high volume — means the market has strong conviction, not just noise.
Vol/liquidity ratio (20pts)
24h volume is more than 3× the available liquidity — means large traders are moving the book.
Urgency (15pts)
Market closes within 7 days — late surges in closing markets are a classic signal of informed positioning.
Absolute size (10pts)
Raw 24h volume over $100K — pure scale, because small trades don't move markets.
A high conviction score does not mean insider knowledge is confirmed. Volume spikes have many innocent explanations — breaking news, influencer posts, or coordinated retail activity. The hypothetical returns shown on each card reflect what a $1,000 position would pay out if the market resolves in that direction, not a prediction of what will happen. This is not financial advice.
